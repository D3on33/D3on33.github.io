01 — Context & Role

Redesigning onboarding for the largest independent broker-dealer in the US

This engagement was delivered through Incedo Inc., where I served as UX Designer on LPL Financial's Project Unite — a 0→1 redesign of the advisor onboarding experience. I owned end-to-end UX from research through engineering handoff.

LPL Financial manages over $1 trillion in brokerage and advisory assets. Onboarding a new advisor meant navigating phone calls, fragmented portals, and manual email chains — creating thousands of hours of operational drag annually and a measurable NPS deficit against competitors.

My Contributions
Led UX research, journey mapping, and end-to-end design for digital forms, document repository, scheduling, and real-time workflow tracking. Collaborated with Product Managers and engineers to prioritize features against the NPS gap. Presented design rationale directly to LPL Financial product leadership.
02 — The Problem

1,000+ hours to onboard
a single advisor.

Onboarding at LPL Financial required 2,478 manual actions across phone, email, and a fragmented portal. There was no single source of truth — advisors couldn't see where they were in the process, and LPL staff spent hours on manual coordination that should have been automated.

A 48% advisor dissatisfaction rate and an 8–24pt NPS gap against top competitors made the business cost visible. But the root cause was structural: the process had been patched over time, never designed end-to-end.

The instinct was to digitize the existing process. I pushed to redesign it. Digitizing a broken workflow just makes broken faster.

Redesign over Digitization

03 — Research

Finding the highest-value unlock

Research focused on the full onboarding journey — from initial contact through first productive day — identifying where manual effort could be eliminated without compromising compliance requirements.

01
Advisor Interviews
Moderated sessions with recently onboarded advisors to map the actual journey vs. intended process — surfacing where steps were missed, duplicated, or unclear.
02
Staff Shadowing
Observed LPL onboarding staff to understand the manual coordination burden and identify which steps required human intervention vs. automation.
03
Competitive Benchmarking
Analyzed onboarding flows at competing broker-dealers to understand the NPS gap and identify table-stakes features missing from LPL's current experience.
04
Process Mapping
Mapped all 2,478 manual actions to identify consolidation opportunities — the output became the feature prioritization framework for the entire portal.
Key Insight
Advisors weren't frustrated by the volume of steps — they were frustrated by opacity. They never knew where they were in the process or what was blocking their progress. Visibility was the highest-value unlock, not feature reduction.

04 — Design Process

Visibility-first,
then consolidation

01
Journey Mapping
End-to-end mapping of the advisor onboarding lifecycle — from initial contact through first productive day. Identified 12 handoff points causing friction and delay.
02
Feature Prioritization
Worked with PMs to map features against NPS gap drivers. Digital forms, document repository, scheduling, and real-time tracking emerged as the core four.
03
Prototype & Testing
Interactive prototypes tested with 8 advisors across two rounds. Iterated on form complexity and progress visibility before committing to high-fidelity.
04
High-Fidelity & Handoff
Final Figma designs with annotated specs. Partnered with engineers on compliance requirements for document handling and e-signature flows.

I prioritized real-time workflow tracking above all other features — not because it was easiest, but because it addressed the highest-frustration pain point: advisors not knowing where they stood.

Visibility-First Prioritization
05 — Solution

One portal.
Zero manual coordination.

The redesigned portal gave advisors a single source of truth for the entire onboarding journey. Core features: digital forms with branching logic, a document repository with upload tracking, integrated scheduling, and real-time workflow progress — all in one authenticated experience.

The elimination of phone and email dependencies didn't just reduce hours — it gave advisors agency over their own onboarding timeline for the first time. Status was always visible. Next steps were always clear.

What I'd Do Differently
I'd push for longitudinal satisfaction tracking at 30, 60, and 90 days post-onboarding — not just at portal handoff. The real test of an onboarding redesign is whether advisors feel productive faster, not just whether they complete the process faster. Those are different measurements.

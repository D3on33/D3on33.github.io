01 — Context & Role

02 — The Problem

A digital acquisition workflow
built on printed flyers.

Bankers at US Bank were running client acquisition almost entirely on printed materials. Digital tools existed but were siloed, slow to update, and couldn't be shared dynamically. Personalization at scale was impossible — every client received the same generic content regardless of product fit.

Measurable friction existed at multiple funnel stages: from initial banker-client contact through application completion. Drop-off was highest at the handoff between physical and digital touchpoints.

I reframed the problem from "how do we digitize print" to "how do we make digital faster than print." Speed of use — not digital literacy — was the real barrier.

Strategic Pivot

"Bankers couldn't store anything digitally — every client touchpoint started with a printer."

03 — Research

Surfacing the real barrier

Research focused on understanding friction across the banker–client acquisition journey. Methods were chosen to surface behavioural patterns, not surface preferences.

Key Insight
Bankers weren't avoiding digital tools because they preferred print. They avoided them because the tools required more steps than just printing a flyer. Speed of use was the real barrier, not digital literacy.
01
Stakeholder Interviews
In-depth sessions with bankers, branch managers, and product owners to map current workflow and identify organizational constraints.
02
Contextual Inquiry
Observed bankers during real client interactions to see where printed materials created friction and which digital tools were avoided in practice.
03
Competitive Analysis
Benchmarked peer financial institutions' acquisition patterns, identifying a 3-step redundancy in the legacy entry flow.
04
Affinity Mapping
Synthesized findings across all streams into clustered insights in Miro — driving feature prioritization and IA decisions with the full product team.
04 — Design Process

System-first, then screens

01
Information Architecture
Restructured platform IA for banker-facing and customer-facing views with role-based access logic built in from the start.
02
Wireframing & Concept Testing
Rapid iterations tested with 6 bankers across two rounds. Validated navigation and personalization flow before visual design.
03
Multi-BU Design System
White-labeled Figma system. Each BU deployment a configuration, not a rebuild. Architected before high-fidelity screens.
04
High-Fidelity & Handoff
Interactive prototypes with annotated specs. Worked with dev leads to QA component behavior and edge states pre-release.

I chose to build the design system before high-fidelity screens — not after. This forced alignment on patterns early and eliminated weeks of rework as we scaled across business units.

System-First Decision
05 — Solution

From printed flyers to a
personalized digital engine

The redesigned platform gave bankers a fully digital acquisition toolkit. Core shift: from static printed materials to a dynamic, personalized content engine that met clients where they were.

Key features included a personalized recommendation engine surfacing relevant products by client profile, multi-channel delivery in 2 clicks via Email, QR, or SMS, and a white-labeled design system making each BU deployment a configuration — not a rebuild.

What I'd Do Differently
I'd push for longitudinal measurement beyond the initial launch window. 30-day post-launch metrics told one story — but how adoption patterns held at 6–12 months as bankers fully internalized the workflow would have been the more compelling data for continued investment.
